My Plans

What inspired me to pursue a degree and a career in cyber-security was a conversation I had with a soon-to-retire lecturer at West Lothian College.

Sadly, I've forgotten the gentleman's name however his description of the Tuesday "hacking club" sold the course to me. I thought "You're going to show me how to hack into computers? You had my curiosity, now you have my attention!"

Some elements of the course were full on. Learning IP addressing systems, binary calculations, basic Python scripting and networking were all modules I had to work hard get to grips with. Other modules like professionalism and ethics were drier but I enjoy writing and considering ethical and moral dilemmas so I had no problem meeting the module requirements.

What I really enjoyed about the HNC in Cyber-Security was in playing with Kali Linux, password cracking with John the Ripper, exploiting a vulnerability on a Windows XP machine plus the Digital Forensic work with tools like Wireshark and Autopsy.

Hacking into computers and to a slightly lesser degree, Digital Forensics as a possible career path perked my interest in a way nothing had before. I haven't closed myself to other career paths but I feel inspired to pursue Penetration Testing, Digital Forensics and Incident Response as a career path
