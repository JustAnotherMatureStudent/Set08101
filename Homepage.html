When I was supporting the Napier Student Fair on behalf of my employer, I got chatting to some guys who were doing a user experience degree. I asked them for some user-experience tips that might help me meet the needs of this assessment. They asked who the website was for. I said "My lecturer".

After we laughed and talked some more I gave it some more thought. Who is this website for?

The theme for this website is food. Speaking as someone who has a healthy appetite, it's not difficult for me to think about or eat food - producing a website for it however is something else entirely.

That being said, the website is for me and people like me. People who lead busy lives and are short on time.
For instance, I'm short on time because I'm a dad, a husband and a Cyber-Security Graduate Apprentice which means that as well as my studies I also have a full-time job.

In addition to those roles, I like to go to the gym to work out the stress of the day as well as keep myself active and strong.

While I don't consider myself old, I don't consider myself young either and I want to I want to remain as fit and strong as I can so I can keep up with my daughter as she grows up.

As parents, my wife and I want our daughter to eat healthily and we recognise the value of eating together as a family however it is a constant challenge to find the time to do each of these things.

The solution I've settled upon to meet this challenge is slow cooking a meal that requires as little preparation as possible and as few finishing touches as possible. the closer I can get to a one pot meal that is served straight from the slow cooker, the better!

As well as that though, I want a slow cooking website for people who are short on time to be simple to navigate - you shouldn't spend any more time than is necessary to get the informationy you need to make your slow cooked meal

If you're short on time and want to have a meal on the table for you and your kids after a busy day, this could be the website for you
